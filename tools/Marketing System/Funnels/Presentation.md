# Presentation Funnels: Belief-Shifting at Scale

**Core Insight:** Presentation funnels (webinars, VSLs, masterclasses) are the most powerful conversion tool for mid-to-high ticket offers. They allow extended belief transformation that can't happen in a short ad or email. A well-structured presentation can convert cold audiences to buyers in a single session.


## Objective

To provide a framework for building presentation funnels that drive registrations, maximize show rates, and convert viewers into buyers through structured belief transformation.

## 1. Presentation Funnel Types

### Live Webinar
- **Format:** Scheduled, real-time presentation
- **Advantages:** Highest energy, live Q&A, maximum urgency
- **Challenges:** Scheduling constraints, show-rate variability
- **Best For:** Launches, high-ticket, engaged audiences

### Automated/Evergreen Webinar
- **Format:** Pre-recorded, simulated live experience
- **Advantages:** Runs 24/7, scalable, consistent
- **Challenges:** Lower urgency, can feel inauthentic
- **Best For:** Evergreen funnels, consistent lead flow

### Video Sales Letter (VSL)
- **Format:** Direct-to-video sales page, no registration
- **Advantages:** No show-rate problem, immediate viewing
- **Challenges:** Shorter attention, no pre-registration commitment
- **Best For:** Lower-ticket offers, warm traffic

### Masterclass/Workshop
- **Format:** Deeper training, often multi-session
- **Advantages:** More belief-shifting time, higher value
- **Challenges:** Higher commitment required
- **Best For:** Premium positioning, complex offers

## 2. Webinar Funnel Structure

### Complete Flow
```
Traffic → Registration Page → Thank You + Calendar → Reminder Sequence
                                                           ↓
                               Live/Replay Presentation → Order Page → Tier-Ups → Thank You
                                                           ↓
                                          Follow-Up Sequence (Buyers + Non-Buyers)
```

### Page 1: Registration Page
**Purpose:** Convert traffic to registrants

**Elements:**
- Headline: Clear promise/outcome
- Subheadline: What they'll learn
- Bullet points: Specific takeaways (3-5)
- Presenter credibility
- Date/time (live) or "Watch Now" (evergreen)
- Registration form (name, email)
- Scarcity indicator (spots, timing)

### Page 2: Thank You / Confirmation
**Purpose:** Confirm registration, maximize show rate

**Elements:**
- Confirmation message
- Add to calendar links
- Expectation setting
- Pre-webinar content (video, PDF)
- Social sharing (optional)

### Reminder Sequence
**Purpose:** Get them to show up

**Timing:**
- Immediately after registration
- 24 hours before
- 1 hour before
- "Starting now" (live)

**Content:**
- Remind of value
- Build anticipation
- Overcome "should I attend" objections
- Pre-frame key beliefs

### Page 3: Presentation Page
**Purpose:** Deliver presentation, drive conversion

**Elements:**
- Video embed (live or recorded)
- Chat/Q&A (live)
- CTA button (appears at pitch)
- Timer (countdown to offer or close)

### Page 4: Order Page
**Purpose:** Complete purchase

**Elements:**
- Offer summary
- Price with value anchor
- Guarantee
- Payment options
- Order bumps (if applicable)

## 3. VSL Funnel Structure

### Simplified Flow
```
Traffic → VSL Page → Order Form → Tier-Ups → Thank You → Follow-Up
```

### VSL Page Elements
- Video player (often with no controls initially)
- Headline above video
- CTA button (appears at pitch point)
- Bullet points or summary below video
- Testimonials
- Guarantee

### VSL Advantages
- No show-rate problem
- Immediate consumption
- Easier to test and iterate
- Works well with retargeting

## 4. Presentation Content Structure

See `Persuasion/Conversion-Experiences.md` for full Conversion Theater framework.

### Summary Structure

**1. Introduction (5-10%)**
- Pattern interrupt
- Big promise
- Credibility
- Future pacing
- Master Conviction statement

**2. Three Secrets (60-70%)**
- Secret 1: Break Method barrier
- Secret 2: Break Self barrier
- Secret 3: Break Circumstance barrier
- Each powered by Revelation Arc story

**3. Transition (5%)**
- Recap transformation
- "I created something to help you..."
- Shift to offer

**4. Stack and Close (15-20%)**
- Present offer components
- Build value stack
- Reveal price with anchor
- Add bonuses
- Scarcity and urgency
- Guarantee
- CTA

## 5. Show Rate Optimization

### Registration Page Factors
- Specificity of promise
- Perceived value of content
- Clear date/time
- Urgency elements

### Pre-Webinar Engagement
- Reminder emails (see above)
- Pre-webinar content consumption
- Community engagement
- SMS reminders (if permitted)

### Show Rate Benchmarks
- Live webinar: 25-40%
- Automated webinar: 15-30%
- Masterclass/workshop: 40-60% (higher commitment)

### Replay Strategy
- Send replay link to no-shows
- Time-limited replay (48-72 hours)
- Replay-specific follow-up sequence

---

## 6. Live vs. Evergreen Considerations

### Live Advantages
- Higher energy and engagement
- Real Q&A builds trust
- True urgency (happening now)
- Can adjust based on audience

### Evergreen Advantages
- Runs continuously
- Scalable (no scheduling)
- Consistent experience
- Easier to optimize

### Hybrid Approach
- Run live launches
- Record best performance
- Use recording for evergreen
- Periodic live events for energy spikes

## 7. Follow-Up Sequences

### For Non-Buyers
**Goal:** Continue belief transformation, offer second chances

**Structure:**
- Replay access (time-limited)
- Objection handling emails
- Case studies and testimonials
- FAQ and common questions
- Final deadline email
- "Cart closed" and what's next

### For Buyers
**Goal:** Confirm purchase, begin onboarding

**Structure:**
- Purchase confirmation
- Access and getting started
- First quick win guidance
- Community invitation
- Tier-up or next offer (after consumption)

## 8. Measurement Framework

### Registration Metrics
- Registration page conversion rate
- Cost per registration
- Registration source performance

### Attendance Metrics
- Show rate (registered to attended)
- Watch time / completion rate
- Peak attendance moment
- Drop-off points

### Conversion Metrics
- Attendee-to-buyer conversion rate
- Revenue per registrant
- Revenue per attendee
- Replay conversion rate

### Follow-Up Metrics
- Email open/click rates
- Delayed conversions (after initial presentation)
- Total campaign revenue

## Quality Checks for Presentation Funnels

Before launching:

- [ ] Registration page promise is specific and compelling
- [ ] Reminder sequence is loaded and tested
- [ ] Presentation follows Conversion Theater structure
- [ ] All three belief barriers addressed
- [ ] Offer stack is clear and valuable
- [ ] Order page functions properly
- [ ] Follow-up sequences ready (buyers + non-buyers)
- [ ] Tracking covers full funnel
