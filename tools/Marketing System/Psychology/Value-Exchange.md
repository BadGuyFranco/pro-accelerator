# Value Exchange

The psychology of value perception and commitment.

## The Value Exchange Principle

Marketing is not about extraction; it's about exchange. Every transaction should leave both parties better off.

Customers ascend through increasing value and commitment. Each step should feel like the obvious next move.

## How Value Perception Works

### The Value Equation

```
Perceived Value = (Dream Outcome × Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time to Result × Effort Required)
```

**To increase perceived value:**
- Increase the dream outcome (bigger transformation)
- Increase likelihood (proof, guarantees, support)
- Decrease time (speed of results)
- Decrease effort (ease of implementation)

### Value is Relative

Value is always judged in context:

| Comparison Point | Effect on Value Perception |
|------------------|---------------------------|
| Competitor prices | If you're cheaper, you seem less valuable |
| Alternative solutions | If alternatives are painful, you seem more valuable |
| Cost of inaction | If staying the same is expensive, you seem more valuable |
| Prior investments | If they've already invested in the problem, you seem more valuable |

Control the comparisons your prospect makes.

### Value vs. Price

| Concept | Definition |
|---------|------------|
| **Price** | What they pay |
| **Cost** | What they give up (money, time, effort) |
| **Value** | What they receive (transformation, outcomes) |
| **Worth** | Their subjective assessment of value vs. cost |

**Goal:** Make worth feel disproportionately high.

## The Ascension Architecture

Customers move through a progression of value and commitment. See `Core/Core-Principles.md` for the full Ascension Ladder.

### Ascension Logic

Each step should:
- Solve a problem created or revealed by the previous step
- Deliver a meaningful win
- Reveal a new, larger desire
- Lead naturally to the next rung

**The test:** Does saying yes to this step make the next step more attractive?

### Tier-Ups and Tier-Downs

**Tier-Up:** Higher-priced option with more value, speed, or access
- "Want it faster? Add implementation support."
- "Want more access? Upgrade to the VIP version."

**Tier-Down:** Lower-priced alternative for those who decline
- "Not ready for the full program? Start with the essentials."

Purpose: Capture customers at multiple price points; increase average cart value.

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring

The first number presented influences all subsequent judgments.

**Application:**
- Show total value before price
- Compare to more expensive alternatives
- Show "normal price" before discount
- Break into small increments ("$3/day")

### Price Positioning

| Position | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Lowest in market | Signals low quality |
| Middle of market | Safe but undifferentiated |
| Premium | Signals high quality, attracts serious buyers |
| Highest | Signals exclusivity, repels price shoppers |

Price is a signal. What do you want your price to communicate?

### Payment Psychology

- Multiple payments feel smaller than lump sum
- "Investment" frames better than "cost"
- Payment plans reduce perceived barrier

## Commitment Ladders

People prefer gradual commitment increases over large jumps.

### The Commitment Sequence

```
Free → Low ($) → Medium ($$) → High ($$$) → Ongoing
```

Each step should feel like the obvious next move, not a leap.

### Micro-Commitments

Before asking for purchase, ask for smaller commitments:
- Email opt-in
- Quiz completion
- Webinar attendance
- Engaging with content

Each commitment increases likelihood of next commitment (consistency principle).

## Value Delivery

Value perception extends beyond the sale.

### Onboarding Value

The experience immediately after purchase affects:
- Buyer's remorse prevention
- Momentum toward results
- Likelihood of ascension
- Referral behavior

Deliver visible value immediately. Don't make them wait.

### Ongoing Value

For continuity offers:
- Regular value delivery keeps perceived value high
- Value must exceed monthly/annual cost
- Engagement correlates with retention
- Community and connection add non-replicable value

## Measurement

### Leading Indicators
- Add-to-cart rate
- Tier-up acceptance rate
- Time on offer page
- Questions asked about offer

### Lagging Indicators
- Conversion rate at each price point
- Average cart value
- Customer lifetime value
- Refund rate

### Value Diagnostic

If conversions are low:
1. Is the transformation clear and desirable?
2. Is the vehicle believable?
3. Are deliverables tangible and valuable?
4. Are bonuses addressing real objections?
5. Is risk sufficiently reversed?
6. Is price anchored appropriately?

For offer construction frameworks, see `Persuasion/Offer-Design.md`.
