# Short-Form Video: Attention in Seconds

**Core Insight:** Short-form video is the dominant discovery engine of modern marketing. It rewards pattern interrupts, emotional resonance, and rapid value delivery. Master the first second, and you earn the next; fail it, and you're scrolled past forever.


## Objective

To provide principle-based guidance for creating short-form video content that captures attention, builds audience, and drives traffic to owned assets, regardless of which platform dominates.

## 1. Platform Psychology: Why People Are Here

### The Mindset
- **Entertainment-First:** Users seek dopamine, not education
- **Passive Discovery:** They didn't search for you; you interrupted them
- **Low Commitment:** Swiping away costs nothing
- **Pattern-Seeking:** Brains crave novelty within familiarity

### What This Means for Marketing
- Value must be immediate, not promised
- Entertainment and education must blend
- You're competing with infinite alternatives
- Recognition (naming their experience) beats information

---

## 2. Attention Patterns: How Content Gets Consumed

### The First-Second Rule
The first second determines everything:
- Visual pattern interrupt (movement, contrast, unexpected)
- Audio hook (voice, sound, music)
- Text overlay (curiosity, call-out, bold claim)

**Principle:** If you don't earn the first second, nothing else matters.

### The Watch-Time Equation
Algorithm favor = completion rate + engagement

| Signal | Weight | How to Optimize |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Watch time | High | Earn each second; no slow intros |
| Completion rate | High | Keep it as short as the value requires |
| Replays | High | Create "wait, what?" moments |
| Shares | High | Make it shareable (relatable, useful, surprising) |
| Comments | Medium | Ask questions, spark reactions |
| Saves | Medium | Provide reference value |

### Content Consumption Modes
- **Sound On:** Design for audio (voice, music, effects)
- **Sound Off:** Design for visual (captions, text overlays, expressions)
- **Best Practice:** Optimize for both simultaneously

## 3. Hook Types for Short-Form

Adapt core hook architecture for the format:

### Visual Hooks
- **Movement:** Physical action in first frame
- **Contrast:** Unexpected visual element
- **Face:** Human expressions capture attention
- **Text:** Bold claim overlaid immediately

### Audio Hooks
- **Voice Inflection:** Energy, curiosity, urgency in first words
- **Sound Effects:** Pattern interrupts that stop the scroll
- **Music:** Trending sounds carry discovery advantage

### Opening Line Formulas
- "Stop scrolling if you..." (call-out)
- "The reason your [X] isn't working..." (problem)
- "I spent [time/money] learning this..." (credibility + curiosity)
- "Nobody talks about this but..." (exclusivity)
- "Here's why [common belief] is wrong..." (contrarian)

---

## 4. Content Structures That Work

### The Mini-Revelation
1. Hook (problem or curiosity)
2. Insight (the "aha")
3. Proof or example (credibility)
4. CTA or open loop (next step)

### The Story Snippet
1. "I was [relatable situation]..."
2. "Then I discovered..."
3. "Now I [result]..."
4. Implication: You can too

### The List Format
1. "3 things you need to know about [X]"
2. Point 1 (fastest value first)
3. Point 2
4. Point 3 (strongest, creates save/share)

### The Myth-Buster
1. "Everyone says [common belief]"
2. "But here's what actually works..."
3. Evidence or example
4. Reframe

---

## 5. Trust Signals in Short-Form

### What Builds Credibility
- **Specificity:** Concrete details over vague claims
- **Demonstration:** Show, don't just tell
- **Vulnerability:** Admitting mistakes or struggles
- **Consistency:** Showing up regularly
- **Social Proof:** Comments, duets, stitches, shares

### What Destroys Credibility
- Over-promising ("I made $1M in 30 days")
- Obvious sales pitch without value
- Inauthentic energy or scripted delivery
- Inconsistent posting (ghost then flood)

---

## 6. Conversion Paths: Attention to Owned Assets

Short-form is discovery, not conversion. The goal is to move audience to owned platforms.

### Primary Conversion Paths
1. **Bio Link:** "Link in bio for [specific thing]"
2. **Comment Prompt:** "Comment [X] for the full guide"
3. **Profile Follow:** "Follow for more [topic]"
4. **DM Trigger:** "DM me [keyword] for..."

### Lead Magnet Integration
- Reference a specific, valuable resource
- Make it relevant to the video content
- Keep the CTA simple and direct
- Track which videos drive conversions

### The Content-to-Funnel Bridge
```
Short-Form Discovery → Profile Visit → Bio Link → Lead Magnet → Email List
                                                              ↓
                                                      Nurture → Sale
```

---

## 7. Sustainability and Batching

### Volume Reality
Short-form rewards volume. Plan for sustainability:
- **Batch Creation:** Film multiple videos in one session
- **Repurposing:** One idea becomes multiple formats
- **Templates:** Repeatable structures reduce creative load
- **80/20 Rule:** Double down on what works

### Content Calendar Principles
- Test multiple formats and angles
- Identify winning content types
- Create series and recurring themes
- Balance evergreen and trending content

---

## 8. Measurement Framework

### Leading Indicators
- Views (are you getting distribution?)
- Watch time / completion rate (is content compelling?)
- Profile visits (are viewers curious about you?)

### Conversion Indicators
- Bio link clicks (are they taking action?)
- Follower growth rate (are they committing?)
- Lead magnet conversions from platform

### Content Performance
- Which hooks perform best?
- Which topics resonate?
- Which formats drive action?
- Which CTAs convert?

---

## Quality Checks for Short-Form

Before posting:

- [ ] First second earns attention (visual + audio hook)
- [ ] Value delivered within the video (not just promised)
- [ ] Optimized for sound-on AND sound-off
- [ ] Clear CTA or next step
- [ ] Consistent with brand voice and positioning
- [ ] Not overly salesy; entertainment-first
